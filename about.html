Steve Holzheauser

Steve Holzheauser, founding member of the Woodhouse Group Texas, has over twenty five years of experience in state politics and government and was a member of the Texas State House of Representatives from 1987 until his retirement in 1999. While in the Texas Legislature, Steve held the position of Chairman of the House Energy Resources Committee overseeing all aspects of the oil and gas industry for two terms. Additionally, Steve served on the powerful Ways & Means, General Calendars, Natural Resources, Economic Development, and Environmental Affairs Committees. During this time, he oversaw many diverse issues including energy policy, telecommunications, environmental issues, tax policy, economic development and water policy to name just a few.

Steve began his government affairs consulting practice in 1999 and has represented client’s in a wide range of policy areas. Steve possesses an in-depth knowledge of the legislative process and maintains broad relationships with key members of the Texas Legislature, the Governor’s office, and state agencies. While foremost understanding the aspirations of his client, Steve has guided difficult and complicated legislative initiatives to the Governor’s desk

A veterinarian by education, Steve received his Bachelor of Science and Doctor of Veterinary Medicine from Texas A&M University.

Angie Cervantes

Angie Cervantes brings over nine years of experience in political affairs and public policy, a thorough understanding of the legislative process, and well-established relationships with members of the legislature, agencies, and their staffs. She served as a policy analyst for the Senate Transportation and Homeland Security Committee and as Deputy Committee Director for the Senate Business and Commerce Committee. During this time she advised State Senator John Carona on, and developed an expertise in, the areas of transportation, occupational regulation, alcohol regulation, energy, and telecommunications.

A native of Eagle Pass, Texas, Angie holds a BA in Government and English from the University of Texas at Austin and a Master of Public Affairs from the LBJ School of Public Affairs at the University of Texas at Austin. She is an avid fan of Longhorn sports, running, yoga, and cooking.

Craig Holzheauser

Craig Holzheauser’s political experience spans both elective and legislative politics. Craig began his career in politics working on various Texas Republican primaries, which allowed him to hone his grassroots and outreach skills. He later went to work for State Representative Myra Crownover, serving first as legislative director and then committee director of the House Committee on Public Health. Craig’s experiences both outside and inside the Texas Capitol give him a unique perspective to the legislative process and make him a valuable asset to the team.

Craig received a Bachelor’s of Science in Construction Science from Texas A&M University.
