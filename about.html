Steve Holzheauser

Steve Holzheauser has over twenty years of experience in state politics and government. A former member of the Texas State House of Representatives, Steve served six terms before retiring from the Legislature in 1999. As a member of the Legislature, Steve held the position of Chairman of the House Energy Resources Committee for two terms, in addition to his service on the powerful Ways & Means, General Calendars, Natural Resources, Economic Development, and Environmental Affairs Committees. After retiring from the Legislature, Steve began his government affairs consulting practice in 1999.

Having represented clients on a diverse range of issues, Steve possesses in-depth knowledge of the legislative process and maintains relationships with key members of the Texas Legislature, the Governor’s office, and state agencies. Steve also understands the inner-workings of bill management through his experience on the House Calendars Committee.

Prior to his career in Texas politics, Steve owned and operated a successful veterinary practice in Houston. He also has managed family farming interests in and around Jackson and Victoria counties, and manages and owns various properties in Central and Southeast Texas. Steve received his Bachelor of Science and Doctor of Veterinary Medicine from Texas A&M University.

Angie Cervantes

Angie Cervantes brings over nine years of experience in political affairs and public policy, a thorough understanding of the legislative process, and well-established relationships with members of the legislature, agencies, and their staffs. She served as a policy analyst for the Senate Transportation and Homeland Security Committee and as Deputy Committee Director for the Senate Business and Commerce Committee. During this time she advised State Senator John Carona on, and developed an expertise in, the areas of transportation, occupational regulation, alcohol regulation, energy, and telecommunications.

A native of Eagle Pass, Texas, Angie holds a BA in Government and English from the University of Texas at Austin and a Master of Public Affairs from the LBJ School of Public Affairs at the University of Texas at Austin. She is an avid fan of Longhorn sports, running, yoga, and cooking.
